Legal Protection & Welfare

Promoting dignity, respect, and legal security for parents and senior citizens.

The Maintenance and Welfare of Parents and Senior Citizens Act, 2007

While society values and respects its elders, there are unfortunate instances where senior citizens face neglect or abuse from their own families. To ensure strict legal protection, the Government of India enacted this landmark legislation.

This Act makes it a legal obligation for children and heirs to provide maintenance to senior citizens and parents, ensuring they can lead a normal, dignified life.

Key Rights & Provisions

  • Right to Maintenance: Parents (biological, adoptive, or step-parents) can claim a monthly maintenance amount from their adult children if they are unable to maintain themselves.
  • Protection of Property: If a senior citizen has transferred their property to an heir on the condition that they will provide basic amenities, and the heir refuses to do so, the transfer can be declared legally void by a tribunal.
  • Speedy Justice (No Lawyers Required): Special Maintenance Tribunals have been established in every sub-division to ensure cases are resolved within 90 days. To keep it simple and free for seniors, lawyers are strictly not permitted to represent parties in these tribunals unless specifically allowed by the Tribunal.
  • Protection Against Abandonment: Abandoning a senior citizen is a criminal offense punishable by imprisonment and fines.
